STUDENT ENGAGEMENT & OUTCOMES TRACKER — STRATEGIC SUMMARY REPORT
======================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This report synthesizes data from 300 student participants across 5 semesters
and 4 experiential learning programs. Findings draw on quantitative pre/post
survey data, attendance tracking, and qualitative open-response analysis.

KEY FINDINGS
------------
1. LEARNING GAINS: Students showed an average overall gain of 1.02 points
   (on a 1–5 scale) across confidence, skill readiness, and engagement metrics.
   The strongest semester was Spring 2024; gains were lowest in Fall 2022,
   suggesting a possible program delivery or cohort composition factor worth investigating.

2. PROGRAM PERFORMANCE: 'Career Readiness' consistently produced the highest
   learning gains. Cross-program replication of its delivery model is recommended.

3. PERSISTENCE & OUTCOMES: 57.0% of students persisted through
   at least half of program sessions. Among persisters, 39.0% secured
   an internship or job — indicating a strong link between program engagement and
   career outcomes.

4. QUALITATIVE THEMES: The most frequently cited theme across open responses was
   'Program Structure & Resources' (34.0% of responses). Sentiment analysis
   shows 225 positive, 9 neutral, and 66 negative responses.
   22.0% of responses flagged areas for improvement — primarily around
   scheduling flexibility and access to advising resources.

5. EQUITY LENS: First-generation students showed an average overall gain of 1.02
   vs. 1.01 for non-first-gen peers (gap: -0.01).
   No significant equity gap detected — positive sign for program inclusivity.

RECOMMENDATIONS
---------------
• Scale delivery elements from the highest-performing program to other pathways.
• Introduce mid-semester check-in surveys to improve early identification of at-risk students.
• Add flexible scheduling options and expand advising touchpoints (flagged in 'Areas for Improvement').
• Develop targeted outreach for first-generation students in lower-performing semesters.
• Establish a semester-over-semester dashboard review cadence with program leads.

DATA NOTES
----------
• 0 records flagged for missing Likert data (excluded from gain calculations).
• Qualitative themes extracted via keyword matching; manual review recommended for nuance.
• All data is synthetic and for demonstration purposes only.
